Level sensor


The level sensor is intended for measurement liquids level and fluidized substances such as granular materials or powders. The sensor should be immersed in substance to measure the level of substance inside container. But there are also level sensors that can work for example to check level of river.There are two ways to define the level of liquid. There could be continuously checked value and determine the exact amount of the substance in specified range. Another way is point level measurement that indicates whether the substance is above or below the sensing point.

Point level detection sensors:

  • Magnetic and mechanical float,
  • Pneumatic,
  • Conductive,
  • Optical interface,
  • Microwave,

Continuous level measurement sensors:

  • Magnetostrictive,
  • Resistive chain,
  • Magnetoresistive,
  • Hydrostatic pressure,
  • Air bubbler,
  • Gamma ray,

Among level sensors there are also ones that operates on both continuous monitoring and point level detection:

  • Ultrasonic,
  • Capacitance,
  • Optical interference,
  • Air bubbler,
  • Microwave

For selection of level measurement method could affect multiple variables, such as: phase, temperature, pressure, vacuum, chemistry, dielectric constant of medium, density of medium, agitation, noise, vibration, mechanical shock, tank or bin size and shape.

To use level sensor there is also different criteria to consider:

  • Price,
  • Accuracy,
  • Appearance,
  • Response rate,
  • Ease of calibration or programing,
  • Physical size and mounting of device,
  • Monitoring or control of continuous or discrete (point) levels.

Application:

  • Liquids,
  • Powered and granular solids,
  • Liquid metals at very high temperature,
  • Corrosive materials,
  • Very high pressure industrial process.

Capacitance level sensor

The principle of level sensor is to convert a change in position or properties of the dielectric material into an electric signal. Those sensors are operated by varying of capacitance parameters, which are: distance between electrodes, area of capacitive plates and dielectric constant:


Capacitive level sensor can be operated in two ways:

  • by 2 electrodes forming a capacitor placed in container in a form of metal plates with insulating (dielectric) material between them. This type of electrode is used for non-metallic tanks,

  • by one electrode when the metal tank wall acts as other electrode.

The shape of electrode can be different. The substance plays the role of dielectric and fill the gap between the electrodes. The changes of substance level affect the changes of the capacitance. To measure the capacitance variation, electric energy that flows into and out of the electrodes is measured as voltage changes. Empty tank will have lover capacitance than filled up.

Measurement

When the substance is introduced between electrodes of the capacitor the capacitance changes progressively and liquid level can be determined. To measure the variation in capacitance electric energy flowing into and out of electrodes is measured as the circuit potential is varied. The regular flow of energy is established by connecting electrodes to alternating current measurement circuit. The more energy the higher the capacitance and more dielectric between electrodes.
To the capacitor circle is applied a radio frequency to the capacitance circuit and this method is called as RF level sense. When RF signal (low MHz) is applied, it causes a low current flow between probe and tank wall through dielectric material. The drop of substance level cause drop of the capacitance and minute drop of current flow. The current difference is detected by the capacitor switch and translated into a change in the relay state of the level switch. In the case of continuous level detectors (vertical probes), the output is not a relay state, but a scaled analog signal.

There are three categories of level measurement:

  1. Measurement of non-conductive material– the probe has no insulator because of high liquid resistance make it dielectric,
  2. Measurement of conductive material– electrodes are insulated with thin coating of glass or plastic to avoid short circuit,
  3. Non-contact measurement- fixed area of plates but varied distance between them, this measurement does not produce a linear output and is used when the level varies by several inches.

Advantages of capacitance level sensor:

  • Simple installation,
  • Low cost,
  • Good resolution (as low as 0.003 mm) and frequency response,
  • Up to 100m measurement,
  • Have no moving parts,
  • Can be made for higher temperature and pressure applications,

Disadvantages of capacitance level sensor:

  • Become inaccurate if the measured substance is prone to contamination by agents that change the dielectric constant,
  • Even acids and caustics that do not appear to coat the sensing element are so conductive that the thin film they leave can cause serious errors in measurement,
  • The measurement of capacitance is hard compare to measurement of resistance,
